Description

An alternative approach to testing hypotheses when your variables are skewed is to transform them and conduct a parametric test. Apply the log transformation to the target variable and conduct the parametric hypothesis test to see the difference in the average order value for both control and experimental groups. Is the conclusion on the null hypothesis similar to the one from the previous stage?

Objectives

  • Apply log transformation to order value to make it normal and plot the new distribution;
  • Similar to the first stage, conduct the t-test:
  1. Ð¡heck the assumption for the equality of variances using Levene's test. Print the test statistic, p-value, and conclude the null hypothesis (Reject null hypothesis: yes or no) and variances (Variances are equal: yes or no);
  2. Based on the results of Levene's test, apply the t-test on the independent samples with equal or unequal variances. Print the test statistic and p-value, conclude the null hypothesis (Reject null hypothesis: yes or no) and whether group means are equal or not (Means are equal: yes or no).

Your program must follow the format in the Examples section: first, a histogram, and the test results afterward.

Note that there are only two options: p-value > 0.05 or p-value <= 0.05.

Examples

Variances are equal: yes

T-test

Means are equal: yes
