Description

The A/A test showed a statistically significant difference in the average order value in the two groups. It means that the sampling procedure was incorrect or the group size was too small. The product team had no bias in the sampling — it was random. You should calculate the new sample size that will be sufficient to detect the minimum statistically significant difference.

Objectives

  • Perform a power analysis with the effect of \(0.2\), the power of \(0.8\) and the significance level of \(0.05\) to estimate the sample size of one group. Round the results to the nearest hundredth. Control and experimental groups are supposed to be equal;

  • Upload the resulting dataset and count the number of sessions in the control and experimental groups (the group column).

Your program must follow the format shown in the Example section.

[HINT]You can try the statsmodels.stats.power.tt_ind_solve_power() function from the statsmodels module.[/HINT]

Example

Sample size: 600  # it is the result of power analysis

Control group: 598
Experimental group: 601
